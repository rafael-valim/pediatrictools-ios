Pediatric Tools is a comprehensive suite of 20+ evidence-based clinical calculators designed for pediatric healthcare professionals. All tools work offline, require no account, and collect no data.

SCORING & ASSESSMENT TOOLS
- Ballard Score: Gestational age assessment for newborns
- Apgar Score: Rapid newborn condition evaluation at 1 and 5 minutes
- PEWS (Pediatric Early Warning Score): Early detection of clinical deterioration
- FLACC Score: Pain assessment for pre-verbal and non-verbal patients
- Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS): Consciousness level assessment
- PRAM Score: Preschool Respiratory Assessment Measure for asthma severity
- PECARN Head Injury Rule: CT decision support for pediatric head trauma
- Phoenix Sepsis Score: Pediatric sepsis criteria assessment

CALCULATORS
- Pediatric Drug Dosage: Weight-based medication dosing with common pediatric drugs
- IV Fluid Calculator: Holliday-Segar method for maintenance fluid rates
- Body Surface Area (BSA): Mosteller formula calculation
- Corrected Age: Adjusted age for premature infants
- Growth Percentile: WHO growth charts for weight, height, and head circumference
- Dehydration Assessment: Clinical severity estimation with fluid deficit calculation
- FENa Calculator: Fractional Excretion of Sodium for renal assessment
- ETT Size Calculator: Endotracheal tube sizing by age
- Schwartz GFR: Estimated glomerular filtration rate for children
- QTc Calculator: Corrected QT interval (Bazett formula)
- Blood Pressure Percentile: Age, sex, and height-based BP evaluation
- Bilirubin Risk Assessment: Neonatal jaundice phototherapy thresholds

KEY FEATURES
- 20+ validated clinical tools in one app
- Works completely offline
- Available in English, Portuguese, Spanish, and French
- Clean, intuitive interface optimized for clinical workflow
- Universal app for iPhone and iPad
- No account required, no data collection
- Dark mode support

IMPORTANT: This app is intended as a reference tool for healthcare professionals. It is not a substitute for clinical judgment. Always verify calculations and consult current clinical guidelines.
